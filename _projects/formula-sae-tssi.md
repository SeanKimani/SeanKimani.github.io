## Project Context

As a Controls Team Member for Columbia University Formula SAE, I contributed to the mechanical redesign and integration of the Tractive System Status Indicator, also known as the TSSI. The TSSI is a required safety indicator for the electric vehicle system, so the enclosure design needed to support visibility, durability, packaging, wiring clearance, and compliance with updated FSAE EV safety requirements.

## My Role

I supported enclosure CAD development, mounting strategy, waterproofing design, and iterative prototype validation. My work focused on helping the TSSI assembly fit cleanly within the vehicle packaging constraints while maintaining visibility and reliable mechanical performance.

## Engineering Work

Key engineering considerations included:

- Reducing the external footprint of the enclosure while preserving internal clearance
- Designing around LED visibility and wiring paths
- Supporting sealed interfaces using O-ring and gasket-style features
- Improving fitment and manufacturability through resin-based 3D printing
- Reviewing mechanical alignment and assembly constraints across design iterations

## Outcome

The redesigned TSSI enclosure reduced the external footprint by approximately 25% while supporting updated FSAE EV safety requirements. Across multiple design iterations, the team improved manufacturability, waterproofing consistency, and assembly alignment for a more robust final design.

## Skills Demonstrated

This project strengthened my experience with electromechanical packaging, CAD-driven enclosure design, design iteration, safety-focused engineering, and practical design for manufacturing.
