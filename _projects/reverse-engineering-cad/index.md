## Project Context

This project focused on reverse engineering a complex mechanical toy dump truck assembly. The goal was to understand how the product was designed, document its physical geometry, and recreate a functional CAD assembly that accurately represented the original mechanical system.

## My Role

I disassembled the assembly into its individual components, measured the parts using precision tools, created a master dimensioning spreadsheet, and built a fully parametric SolidWorks model. I also validated the assembly using interference detection and motion analysis.

## Engineering Process

The project required careful attention to:

- Component disassembly and part identification
- Measurement using calipers and thread gauges
- Master dimensioning and GD&T documentation
- Parametric SolidWorks part modeling
- Assembly mates and design intent
- Steering and suspension motion representation
- Interference detection and clearance validation

## Outcome

The final CAD model recreated 7+ unique components and maintained proper mechanical alignment throughout the assembly's range of motion. The model used top-down design intent and advanced mates, including limit-distance features, to simulate realistic steering rack-and-pinion motion and suspension travel.

## Skills Demonstrated

This project demonstrates my ability to move from a physical product to a validated CAD assembly, combining measurement, reverse engineering, mechanical reasoning, and SolidWorks assembly validation.
